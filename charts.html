Charts & data viz

Gray carries the data. The prism makes the point. Every D8A chart makes exactly one point — and that's the series that gets the cobalt.

Chart palette (ordered)

The hero series — the point of the chart — gets the prism: Cobalt 600 (#4536d6) as a solid, or the Prism Fill gradient for a single hero bar. Supporting series get grays, darkest first, never skipping a shade. If no series is the point, the chart is all gray.

Chart rules

  • One prism series per chart. Never two. The cobalt series is the chart's claim — if you can't say which series is the point, none of them gets the gradient.
  • No status baggage. Unlike red or green, cobalt carries no pass/fail meaning — the hero series never gets misread as “good” or “broken”. The brand accent and the chart's own semantic colours never fight.
  • Fills fade. Area charts and the hero bar may use Prism Fade (cobalt 30% → transparent, top to bottom). Supporting series, grids, and axes stay flat gray — depth belongs to the point, not the context.
  • No 3D charts. Ever. Not even for presentations.
  • No chart borders or outlines. Charts float on the page with labels, not boxed frames.
  • Label directly. Put labels on or next to data points. Legends are a last resort.
  • Grid lines: Gray 200 (#e5e5e5), 0.5px weight. Horizontal only. Never vertical grid lines.
  • Axes: Gray 400 (#a3a3a3), 1px weight. Only show axes that carry information.
  • Font: Inter 12px for labels; JetBrains Mono 11px for axis ticks and data values. Never bold in chart text.
  • Bar width: Bars should be wider than the gaps between them. Minimum 60% fill ratio.
  • Pie charts: Avoid. Use horizontal bar charts instead. If you absolutely must use a pie chart, maximum 4 slices.
