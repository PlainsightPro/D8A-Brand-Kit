How to use this kit

The rules that apply to every D8A deliverable. If you're creating anything with our name on it, start here.

The seven rules

01

Ship it clean

Every deliverable uses the correct logo, colors, and fonts. No exceptions. If the template exists, use it.

02

No fluff

If a sentence doesn't say anything, delete it. D8A copy is direct, specific, and useful. Buzzwords are banned.

03

Depth, not gloss

Dark surfaces get depth from tonal gradients and ambient glow — one hue family per gradient, or a fade to transparent (the four recipes in Colors). Still banned: drop shadows, gloss, bevels, multi-hue gradients. Light surfaces stay flat and clean.

04

Monochrome first, red last

Design in black and gray first. If it doesn't work without color, it doesn't work. Then add exactly one Redline statement — the CTA, the number, the line that matters. One weapon per surface.

05

Consistency over creativity

Every D8A deliverable should look like it came from the same team. Reuse patterns. Follow the grid. Don't reinvent.

06

Prove it

Don't claim — show. Every bold statement should have a number, a screenshot, or a customer quote behind it. D8A earns trust by demonstrating, not declaring.

07

Lead dark

Marketing surfaces — website, social, decks — sit on Canvas Depth (black warming into Ink Red). Product screenshots glow on dark; Redline cuts hardest there. The light canvas is for reading: docs, email, long-form.

Pre-send checklist

Logo is the correct variant for this background?
Headings and body in Inter; labels, stats, and code in JetBrains Mono?
Gradients follow the four recipes — single family or fade, no gloss or drop shadows?
Copy is direct and free of buzzwords?
Exactly one Redline statement — no more, no less?
Right canvas — dark for marketing, light for reading?
Every claim is backed by evidence?

When in doubt

Open this brand kit. If what you're making doesn't look like it belongs next to these pages, revisit. The canonical reference is always d8a.pro and this kit.
