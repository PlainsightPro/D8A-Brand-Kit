What changed in v4.0 — Prism

v1.0 was the monochrome chassis. Earlier accents — a polite green, a blunt red — were the wrong shape for what D8A is. v4.0 makes the accent a prism: a cobalt-to-blue gradient on a light pearl canvas. One truth, refracted into a spectrum — exactly what platform-agnostic means.

  • The prism (Cobalt → Blue) — the accent is a gradient, not a hex. #5340e6 → #2e6bff, disciplined to one statement per surface. See Colors.
  • Light-led — the canvas is Pearl (#fbfaff) with an ambient Signal Mesh; Ink is the secondary surface for depth. The brand shows up bright, not dark.
  • Soft & dimensional — rounded corners, frosted glass, soft prism glow. The opposite of blunt; premium, current, confident.
  • The one-weapon rule — kept: exactly one prism statement per surface; mesh, tints, and fades are atmosphere and don’t count.
  • The spec voice & the pulse — JetBrains Mono for labels and stats; the prism pulse traveling the infinity loop; the gradient 8 in the wordmark. See Fonts and Logos.
