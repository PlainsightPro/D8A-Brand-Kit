04 · Logos

Logo

The D8A wordmark, with the prism 8 at its centre. Use the right variant for each background. Respect clear space. Never distort.

Wordmark variants

The 8 is the loop, the source, the prism — it always carries the cobalt gradient. The D and A adapt to the background: ink on light, white on ink. This is the primary mark everywhere, from favicon to billboard.

D8A Mono fallback · one-colour print

Last card: where a single flat colour is required (embossing, one-colour print, fax-grade reproduction), the whole wordmark is set solid — ink on light, white on dark. The gradient is dropped, never faked with a mid-blue.

Infinity icon

The infinity loop is the secondary mark — continuous delivery, request to ship. Stroke only, never filled. On its own it may carry the prism as a gradient stroke.

App icon & avatar

For favicons, app icons, and social avatars, the 8 stands alone on a card — the prism at the smallest legible scale. It reads on light and on ink.

8 App icon · on ink
  • The 8 is the only glyph that carries the prism. Never gradient the D or the A.
  • The gradient runs one way — indigo top-left to blue bottom-right, near 110°. Never re-angle it per placement.
  • Below 16px the gradient can muddy; drop to solid Cobalt 600 (#4536d6) for the 8 at favicon scale.

Clear space

The minimum clear space around the logo is equal to the height of the "8" character in the wordmark. No other element may intrude into this zone.

D8A

The dashed border represents the minimum clear-space boundary.

Minimum size

  • Digital: 24px height minimum for the wordmark
  • Print: 10mm height minimum for the wordmark
  • Icon only: 16px / 6mm minimum

Don't

  • Don't rotate, skew, or distort the logo
  • Don't add drop shadows, outlines, or bevels to the wordmark
  • Don't change the font of the wordmark
  • Don't place the logo on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
  • Don't lock up the wordmark with other brand names or logos
  • Don't gradient any glyph other than the 8, or re-angle the prism per placement
  • Don't fake the gradient with a flat mid-blue — use the true prism, or the solid mono fallback
