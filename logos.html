The signal variant

One glyph carries the identity: the 8 — the loop — set in Redline. This is the compact brand mark for places too small for restraint: avatars, favicons, app icons, social profiles. Everywhere else, the wordmark stays monochrome.

D8A Signal wordmark · dark only
D8A Avatar / app icon · 48px
  • Dark backgrounds only. On light canvases the wordmark stays monochrome — there is no light-canvas signal variant.
  • Only the 8, only Redline. Never another glyph, never another color.
  • Small-format only: avatars, favicons, app icons. Documents, decks, and headers use the monochrome wordmark.

Clear space

The minimum clear space around the logo is equal to the height of the "8" character in the wordmark. No other element may intrude into this zone.

D8A

The dashed border represents the minimum clear-space boundary.

Minimum size

  • Digital: 24px height minimum for the wordmark
  • Print: 10mm height minimum for the wordmark
  • Icon only: 16px / 6mm minimum

Don't

  • Don't rotate, skew, or distort the logo
  • Don't add shadows, outlines, or effects
  • Don't change the font of the wordmark
  • Don't place the logo on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
  • Don't lock up the wordmark with other brand names or logos
  • Don't use the signal variant on light backgrounds or at large scale — it's an avatar mark, not a headline
  • Don't color any glyph other than the 8, and never in any color other than Redline
